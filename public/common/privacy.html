1. GENERAL
1.1 About Vue
Vue Cinemas BV (Vue) is part of Vue International, the largest cinema group outside the United States and one of the major players in the big screen entertainment market. Vue has 21 cinemas in 20 cities in the Netherlands. Vue's mission is to deliver the best choice of content combined with the best technology in the best environment.
1.2 Data protection principles
This privacy statement applies to the use of personal data in relation to our website www.vuecinemas.nl, the Vue App and in our cinemas. In order to use these services and products, we need some information from you. Vue handles personal data with care because your privacy is important to us. Your personal data will only be processed if this is necessary to achieve the purposes as described in this privacy statement. We want to be as transparent and clear as possible about how Vue handles your data. That is why we show in this privacy statement what we use your data for and what rights you have.
1.3 Contact details Vue
Responsible for the use of your personal data is Vue Cinemas BV (Vue) with address Danzigerkade 2D, 1013 AP Amsterdam. If you have any questions or comments, please contact us at this address or at avghelpdesk@vuecinemas.nl.
1.4 Disclaimer text changes
The text of this privacy statement is subject to change. This version was last revised on 25-11-2019. To stay informed of our activities, we advise you to regularly consult this privacy statement.
2 What does Vue do with your personal data?
2.1 At the cinema
2.1.1 Tickets, admission, food and drinks
You can buy a movie ticket at the box office at our cinemas and get something delicious for during the movie. Sometimes we ask you to show proof of identity. We must check this in connection with: * the viewing guide for films for 16 years and older; * ordering alcohol at the bar. A minimum legal age of 18 applies here; * granting certain discounts, such as with a CJP pass, 65+ and a student pass; * using a Vue Movie Pass to access a movie. We check this because we have a legal obligation to do so, to check whether you are entitled to a discount ticket or to check that you are the actual holder of the Movie Pass shown. A copy of the proof of identity is never made. If you pay for a purchase with a debit or credit card, we only store data that cannot be traced back to you. This information is used to process the payment.
2.1.2 Cameras and incidental recordings
There are security cameras in our cinemas to ensure the safety of our staff and to prevent fraud and theft. We keep these images for a maximum of 4 weeks. In the event of incidents, we release the images to the Police and Justice.
During events and premieres, it is possible that camera crews from Vue or third parties make recordings. As a visitor to a cinema, you may be able to appear or be approached for an interview. We use the recordings for promotion and marketing purposes.
2.2 Online (website and app)
Our website www.vuecinemas.nl and the Vue App are facilitated on behalf of Vue by an external website/app developer. All data from the website is stored with an external server provider.
2.2.1 Cookies
A cookie is a small text file that is placed on your computer, tablet or smartphone during your visit to a website. Information is stored in this text file. Some cookies are necessary for the website to function. These are the functional cookies. There are also cookies that are used to analyze and improve the quality of the website. These are analytical cookies. In addition, there are cookies that are useful for you because they recognize you when you visit the website again and they remember, for example, preferences (preferred cinema) so that you do not have to indicate it again and again. These cookies contribute to an increased user convenience for you as a visitor. See our cookie statement for more information about the cookies that can be placed via our website.
2.2.2 E-Ticket
You can buy an e-ticket for the film online. You can do this in two different ways. By means of a registration, this can be done via Facebook or after registering an e-mail address and password. Or by creating a guest account. For a guest account, only an email address is required to be able to email the e-ticket. Please note, without a MyVue account you can no longer view your e-tickets on our website and they will only be emailed to you once (it can end up in your SPAM).
The payment of your purchase is handled by an external payment service provider: Buckaroo. For more information on how Buckaroo handles your personal data, see the privacy statement here: www.buckaroo.nl/privacy statement.
2.2.3 Vue Movie Pass
To purchase a Vue Movie Pass subscription you need a MyVue account. The Vue Movie Pass is always personal. When applying for the pass, we therefore ask you for some identifying information. This data is used to put the digital pass in your MyVue account.
You must be at least 18 years old to subscribe to the Vue Movie Pass. If you are younger than 18, we will ask whether your parents/guardian have agreed to take out the subscription. Customer service and payments are made by a third party.
After termination of a subscription, your data will be kept for as long as is necessary for our work.
2.2.4 MyVue Account
You can create a personal MyVue account to buy an e-ticket or because you want to apply for a Vue Movie Pass. You must be at least 16 years old for a My Vue Account. If you are younger than 16, we will ask whether your parents/guardian have agreed to create an account. In your account you can view your watchlist, your ticket history and tickets for future films. You can also save different preferences for the newsletter. If you create an account via your Facebook account, Vue will have access to your public Facebook data as you have set it yourself on Facebook. Think of your name, e-mail address, gender, date of birth, profile picture and your Facebook ID. Vue has no influence on the data collection by Facebook. We therefore recommend that you read Facebook's privacy statement before using this option. We keep your account information as long as you have a Vue account. You can change or delete data in your account at any time. This can be done by logging into your MyVue account. Under your profile you can download a report of the personal data we have about you. You can also send a request to have your account deleted. This removal request will be processed within 30 days. Sometimes we will still need to keep some data in connection with (legal) obligations, such as accounting retention obligations that Vue has. This can be done by logging into your MyVue account. 2.2.5 Newsletter
We want to keep you informed of interesting news, such as about films, events, contests / competitions, offers, etc. We do this via our newsletter. You will receive this if you have indicated that you wish to receive it in your MyVue account or after registering for the newsletter via our website. The newsletter can be requested by anyone with a MyVue Account. The content of the newsletter can be tailored to your preferences. You manage these preferences yourself in your My Vue account. You can also unsubscribe from the newsletter via your MyVue Account.
2.2.6 Research
We can use your e-mail address to approach you for participation in consumer surveys to improve our services online and in the cinema. These investigations are conducted by Vue or other parties.
2.3 Other
2.3.1 Applicants
You can apply via vacancies on the website. Your application data will be deleted no later than 4 weeks after the application process has ended. During the application process you can always indicate that you no longer wish to be considered for a specific vacancy. We will then delete your application data on request.
2.3.2 Contests and promotions
You can participate in competitions and promotions on your own initiative via various channels. The personal data that is processed hereby is necessary to keep you informed about the results and to be able to pay out prizes if you have won a prize. Your data will only be shared with others if this is necessary for the execution of the competition/promotion.
2.3.3 Business communication
You can contact us via our contact form for, for example, renting a room or organizing an event. We will use this information to handle your request and to maintain business relationship management.
2.3.4 Customer Service
If you contact us because you have something to say, a question or a complaint, it will go to Vue Customer Service. The communication that is conducted in this context is stored for a maximum of 1 year. For the Vue Movie Pass, an external party is involved in handling questions and complaints. We need your name and e-mail address to process your question, complaint or comment. Optionally, you can also provide your phone number.
2.3.5 Other
Vue is subject to legal obligations, which require us to keep your personal data. Think of tax-technical or accounting obligations. We also handle your personal data with care and data will be deleted as soon as we no longer have a (legal) obligation to keep your data.
3 YOUR RIGHTS
You are in charge of your personal data. You have the right to inspect, adjust and delete your personal data. You also have the right to limit the processing of your data and you have the right to portability of your data. If you have a My Vue account, read on. If you do not have a My Vue account but we have your personal data for other reasons, you can email a request to exercise your right to avghelpdesk@vuecinemas.nl. My Vue account Access and adjustment is possible by logging in to your MyVue account. In 'profile' you can see what information we have about you and you can apply changes. You can also download a PDF with your data (=Reporting personal data). You can also send us a removal request via 'profile'. Your MijnVue Account will be deleted within 30 working days unless this is not possible due to legal and tax obligations. It is also only possible to remove a MyVue Account if no active Movie Pass is linked to it. You will receive a message from us as soon as this is done. If you are not satisfied with how we handle your data, you can also contact us via customer service. In addition, you have the right to submit a complaint to a supervisory authority such as the Dutch Data Protection Authority.
